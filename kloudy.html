Kloudy

Information Design

Many people who try to quit smoking transfer from traditional cigarettes to E-cigarettes, vapes or other equivalent products. These new “tools” claim to contain less nicotine and be less harmful to the body. However, without accountability, smokers can be consuming the same, if not more, amount of nicotine. The Kloudy Vape and app act as accountability tools for users, as they provide users their daily nicotine consumption, the frequency of vaping sessions, and the times when they smoke. With all the collected information from the Kloudy vape, the app creates progress reports, hence providing users an effective and measurable way of quitting their habit.

The Problem

Smokers trying to quit their habit through vaping, cannot measure their progress of being less nicotine dependent.

Research

Quantitative Research

Is there a market, if so who?
According to the Canadian Community Health Survey (Statistics Canada, 2018), it is estimated that there are over 5 million current smokers living in Canada as of 2017. From these 5 million smokers, 68.0% of them wanted to quit (Centers for Disease Control and Prevention).
