Creative Case Competition

Marketing and Project Management

This past year I was the co-president of the Institute of Communication Culture & Information Technology (ICCIT) Council. ICCIT Council is the official academic society of the ICCIT program at the University of Toronto. We are responsible for creating and executing relevant events, workshops, and networking opportunities for our students. This year, we created our first ever Creative Case Competition.

The Creative Case Competition gave creative students the opportunity to showcase their designs to industry professionals, connect with other designers in their program, and most importantly land an internship at a top marketing agency.

The Problem

As a CCIT student, I noticed the abundance of case competitions for commerce students and a lack of similar competitions for creative students like myself. I wanted the same opportunities of competing and networking for designers.

Research

Second Year

The idea of a case competition for creative students sparked in my second year. I frequently talked about this concept with classmates and friends. All of their feedback was positive and they agreed that there was a definite market for this event.

Therefore, when I ran for Social and Corporate Social Responsibility Director for ICCIT Council in my second year, I used the event as one of my campaign points. Students enjoyed my campaign and its promises and I fortunately won.


Third Year

In my third year, I put my words into action and created the Graphic Design Contest 2018. The graphic design contest helped us lay down the ground work for the Creative Case Competition 2019.

All creative students were encouraged to create digital artwork that would promote our Gala. Since our Gala was on a boat cruise, the theme of the graphics were ocean related. Once we received all the submissions, we uploaded all the designs to our Facebook page. The design with the most amount of likes in the three days, would win a Google Home Mini.


The Graphic Design Contest ended up being one of ICCIT Council's most popular events, with record high engagement from our CCIT students (over 100 likes on our winning post). Creative students loved this event and I knew I had to continue to refine and cultivate this project.


The Solution

After we had concluded there was a market for the Creative Case Competition. My team and I pitched our idea to a top advertising agency in Canada, Bridgemark Agency. They loved our event and decided to partner with us!


Scope

Participants of the Creative Case Competition, were instructed to create a design for a new drink offered by Radical Road Brewing Co. (a previous Bridgemark client). There were two parts to the Creative Case Competition.

Facebook Voting Period

Designers could either work individually or in groups. The contestants would then create a design for the new drink. The designs would then be posted through ICCIT Council’s Facebook account. On this platform, students could vote for their favourite design. The top three submissions would move on to the pitch.


Pitch Day at Bridgemark

Contestants would then pitch their design, thought process, and competitive audit to Bridgemark Agency representatives. The top contestants would then be chosen and given an interview for a summer internship at their agency.

Marketing Campaign

To attract designers in our program, my Council and I ran an extnesive marketing campaign. We utilized both in-person and social media marketing. We spoke at lectures and created numerous social media posts for MailChimp, Facebook, Instagram, and Youtube.


Result

Final Takeaways

The Creative Case Competition had over 50 competitors and was one of our most popular events of the year. Due to our collective efforts, designers from the team Colt found an internship at a top advertising agency in Canada.


Furthemore, due to this event and other academic events, ICCIT Council won the Academic Excellence Award from the University of Toronto Mississauga Student Union.
