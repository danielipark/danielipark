Overview

An opportunity for young designers

This past year I was the Co-President of the Institute of Communication Culture & Information Technology (ICCIT) Council. ICCIT Council is the official Academic Society of the ICCIT program at the University of Toronto Mississauga.

The Creative Case Competition gave creative students the opportunity to showcase their designs to industry professionals, connect with other designers in their program, and most importantly land an internship at a top branding agency.

The Problem

Young designers in the ICCIT program needed opportunities to grow their network and showcase their design.

Research

Was there an interest from ICCIT students?

The idea of a case competition for creative students sparked in my second year. I frequently talked about this concept with classmates and friends. All of their feedback was positive and they agreed that there was a definite market for this event.

Therefore, when I ran for Social and Corporate Social Responsibility Director for ICCIT Council in my second year, I used the event as one of my campaign initiatives. Students enjoyed my campaign and its promises and I fortunately won.

A poster from my campaign for Social and CSR Director.

Graphic Design Contest

Testing the market

In my third year, I put my words into action and created the Graphic Design Contest 2018. The graphic design contest helped us lay down the ground work for the Creative Case Competition 2019.

All creative students were encouraged to create digital artwork that would promote our Gala. Since our Gala was on a boat, the theme of the graphics were ocean related. Once we received all the submissions, we uploaded all the designs to our Facebook page. The design with the most amount of likes in the three days, would win a Google Home Mini.

The Graphic Design Contest received record high engagement from our CCIT students (over 100 likes on our winning post). Creative students enjoyed this event and I knew I had to continue to refine and cultivate it.

A promotional graphic from our Instagram account.

The Solution

Partnering with a recognized branding agency

After we had concluded there was a market for the Creative Case Competition. My team and I pitched our idea to a top branding agency in Canada, Bridgemark Agency. They loved our event and decided to partner with us!

Bridgemark's notable clients include Tylenol, Schick, and Pedigree.

Based out of Mississauga, Ontario.

Scope

Rundown of the Creative Case Competition

Participants of the Creative Case Competition, were instructed to create a design for a new drink offered by Radical Road Brewing Co. (a previous Bridgemark client).

There were two parts to the Creative Case Competition.

1.Facebook Voting Period

Designs made by contestants were posted on ICCIT Councilâ€™s Facebook account. On this platform, students could vote for their favourite design. The top three submissions moved on to the pitch.

2. Pitch Day at Bridgemark

Contestants pitched their design thought process and competitive audit to Bridgemark Agency representatives. The top contestants were offered an interview for a summer internship at Bridgemark.

Marketing Campaign

Gaining interest from the student body

To attract designers in our program, my Council and I ran an extensive marketing campaign. We utilized both in-person and social media marketing. We spoke at lectures and created numerous social media posts for MailChimp, Facebook, Instagram, and Youtube.

Promotional graphics for the Creative Case Competition from our Instagram account.

Conclusion

Final Takeaways

The Creative Case Competition had over 50 competitors and was one of our most popular events of the year. As a result of our collective efforts, designers from the team Colt were hired at a top advertising agency in Canada.

Furthemore, due to this event and other academic initiatives, ICCIT Council won the Academic Excellence Award from the University of Toronto Mississauga.
