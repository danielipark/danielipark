Quercus

Information Design

Quercus is the official Learning Management System for University of Toronto students. We wanted to redesign this platform to tackle two key problems. Firstly, students were not well aware of the Quercus calendar function due to a poor interface design and personal preferences. Secondly the Quercus inbox was not integrated with UTORmail, despite it being the number one platform choice for students communicating with their professors/TAs.

Our new design focuses on improving these functionalities by increasing the usefulness and efficiency of these tools. Various prototypes were tested by numerous participants and ultimately we were able to come to a final design.

The Problem

The calendar and inbox functionalities of the Quercus system, are not optimized for student use.

Research

Preliminary Observations

My group and I conducted over ten interviews with current UofT students, instructors, and administrators about their thoughts on the Quercus platform.


Direct Quotes

“The Inbox feature on Quercus is quite confusing. I have always used UTORmail to contact my professors and peers. Now that I have an additional method of communication, I don’t know which platform to use. I wish UTORmail was directly integrated into Quercus.”


“I don’t use the Inbox feature at all. I find it useless, as most of my professors want me to contact them through UTORmail, using OutLook. ”


“Some of my professors use the Quercus Inbox and some use the UTORmail inbox. It frustrates me to constantly check both platforms.”

“The Calendar function has so much potential to be a useful feature. However, the Calendar is not up-to-date. Most of my assignment deadlines are not posted in the Calendar, hence why I manually input them in my personal agenda.”


“I cannot rely on the Calendar to check my project deadlines. Deadlines are subject to change in each course and they are not updated to the Calendar. Currently, I am using my Calendar on my MacBook to input these dates.”


“If the Quercus Calendar had an option to sync my Google Calendar, I would definitely use it more.”

Breaking Down the Process

The next step was to put the knowledge from research in motion to define the system's functions, user flows and design the prototype.

Quercus Inbox Functions

  • Communicate to professors, classmates, and peers
  • UTORmail integration
  • Automated contact information

Quercus Calendar Functions

  • Input various different tasks
  • Up-to-date assignment/project deadlines
  • Google calendar and ICal sync option
  • View long-term goals
