Confusion kills curiosity.

When making a big purchase, say a $60,000 car, you want to know everything.

Customers have questions and they want them answered fast. Without shopping expectations being immediately met users will leave. Imagine Pizza Hut being ambiguous about their specials and toppings. Cart abandonment would be through the roof.

Designing for a page that lacks narrative.

I wanted to highlight the key concerns and shortfalls of the current Audi page. Using a content audit and user research, I was able to establish 2 main issues.

1. Low click-through rate to the Used Inventory page.


Customers were not clicking the ‚ÄúView Inventory‚Äù CTA. My ultimate goal is for customers to view used Audi cars and purchase them.

The current page had less than 2% of visitors clicking on the CTA.

2. Surprise! Customers like to see beautiful Audi cars!


I wanted to see if we could leverage user insight from previous Audi redesign projects.


Therefore I initiated an internal conversation with our Senior User Researcher. One sentiment from the conversation was that customers loved large images, videos, and short paragraphs.

The current page had large bodies of text and insignificant images.
